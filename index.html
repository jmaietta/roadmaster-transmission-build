One important correction

Corvette-derived engine. Roadmaster-specific build.

The wagon has the B-body LT1—related to the C4 Corvette engine, but not identical in heads, calibration, output, or vehicle duty. Its original transmission is an early one-piece-case 4L60-E. Build for a heavy 4,500-plus-pound street wagon and its actual engine, axle, tires, and use—not for a Corvette or the later LS truck in the video.

01

Identity before inventory

Confirm exactly what is on the bench

This plan assumes the original-type 1994 B-body 4L60-E behind the LT1. Prove that before buying any year-sensitive hydraulic or electrical component.

Do not buy a broad “1993–2006 4L60-E kit” on faith. A 1994 unit predates the 1995 PWM TCC arrangement and the later 300 mm, removable-bellhousing transmission shown in the video. Pump, valve body, separator plate, gaskets, harness, solenoids, input shaft and converter must form one documented, compatible system.

03

Before teardown

Diagnose the cause—not only the burnt part

A failed friction is evidence. Pressure loss, bore wear, a leaking capsule, cross-leak, restricted cooler flow, wrong calibration, or excess travel may be the cause.

  1. Interview and document.Record when the symptom appears: cold/hot, throttle, range, gear, road speed, TCC command, load and elapsed time.
  2. Scan before disconnecting.Save DTCs, commanded gear, ratio data, temperature, TCC command/slip and force-motor data. A generic modern OBD-II reader is not enough for this 1994 car.
  3. Inspect fluid and driveline.Check level, odor, color, water/coolant, metal, mount, U-joints, yoke, linkage, grounds, connector and cooler-line integrity.
  4. Road-test only if safe.Separate 1-2, 2-3, 3-4, coastdown, engagement and TCC apply/release symptoms.
  5. Pressure-test.Use the 1994 procedure, correct temperature and a known gauge. Compare actual pressure to commanded current.
  6. Preserve evidence.Drain through a clean screen, photograph the undisturbed pan/magnet, and cut the filter open.

No 3rd / 4th or 2-3 flare

Inspect the entire 3-4 oil path: input housing/shaft joint, seals, clutch travel, plates, pump passages, AFL/shift/abuse circuits, separator plate and third-accumulator capsule.

Delayed Drive / Reverse

Check fluid/filter seal, pump volume, regulator/boost circuits, forward/reverse apply circuits, abuse plugs, checkballs, clutch seals and manual-valve position.

Band or shell failure

Inspect drum surface/runout, servo pin/bore/travel, anchors, reaction shell hub/splines, bearings/washers and band-release timing.

TCC shudder / no lockup

Verify 1994 on/off controls, brake input, wiring, pump apply valve, converter, cooler flow, contamination and bore integrity.

06

Vehicle removal

Keep the car stable and the evidence intact

The transmission and converter are heavy, awkward and fluid-filled. Use the factory sequence and rated support equipment.

  1. Make the vehicle safe.Park level, chock wheels, disconnect the battery, raise at approved points and independently verify stability.
  2. Baseline the installation.Photograph harness routing, linkage, brackets, grounds, shields, cooler lines, vent, crossmember and driveshaft orientation.
  3. Drain and cap.Capture fluid for inspection. Cap every open line and transmission port immediately.
  4. Remove connected systems.Follow the 1994 manual for clearance items, driveshaft, linkage, wiring, VSS, lines, starter/access cover and converter bolts.
  5. Support before unbolting.Secure the transmission to the jack and support the engine before mount/crossmember and bellhousing fasteners.
  6. Separate without force.Move straight rearward until the converter clears the flexplate/crank pilot. Keep the converter captured.
  7. Inspect the vehicle side.Check flexplate, pilot, dowels, seal, mount, U-joints/yoke, wiring, grounds, cooler and lines.
Never work under a vehicle supported only by a jack. Never let the converter slide from the transmission, and never allow the engine to hang unsupported.
07

Controlled disassembly

Create a map, not a pile of parts

Photograph every transition and keep each assembly in a covered, labeled tray with its directional thrust components.

  1. Measure first.Record available endplay, converter installation depth and external observations.
  2. Pan and controls.Remove filter, harness, solenoids, pressure switch, accumulators, valve body, plate and both gaskets.
  3. Make physical maps.Trace plate/gaskets, map bolt lengths, and record every checkball by size, material and location.
  4. Servo and pump.Remove servo as an indexed stack. Pull the pump correctly—never pry the case surface.
  5. Front modules.Lift reverse-input drum, input housing, band and thrust parts as discrete groups.
  6. Geartrain.Index shell, sun gear, carriers, ring gear/reaction shaft, output and low/reverse support.
  7. Low/reverse.Use the correct compressor. Capture piston, springs, plates, frictions, selectives and snap rings.
  8. Disassemble modules last.Review evidence, then document clutch order, burn pattern and measured pack thickness.
Keep the “old” parts. Plate, gaskets, springs, checkballs, selectives, washers, snap rings, servo pin and failed frictions remain evidence until the rebuild is proven.
08

The reliability stage

Clean, inspect, measure, test—then order

A new clutch pack cannot compensate for a leaking circuit. Every failed part needs a verified cause and corrective action.

Case and checkball capsulesStructure · bores · sealing surfaces
  • Inspect pump bore, support lugs, snap-ring groove, porosity/cracks and dowels.
  • Inspect servo, accumulator and manual-shaft bores plus valve-body surface flatness.
  • Wet-air/vacuum-test the third-accumulator capsule; it must seal and vent.
  • Prove cooler/lube passages are open.
Pump and stator supportPressure · converter · 3-4 feed
  • Inspect pocket, rotor, vanes, rings, slide, crescent, pivot, depth and flatness.
  • Vacuum-test regulator/boost/TCC circuits and cover/stator separately.
  • Inspect splines, tube, bushings, ring lands and alignment.
  • Wet-air test 3-4/forward passages for cracks or cross-leaks.
  • Reject later PWM/300 mm hardware.
Input housing and 3-4 circuitHighest-priority failure path
  • Inspect shaft joint, ring grooves, turbine shaft, drum splines, feeds and checkballs.
  • Inspect bores, piston/seals, apply/backing plates, snap-ring groove and load-release springs.
  • Read the burn pattern for uneven apply, coning, clearance, leakage or heat.
  • Measure plate flatness, travel around the pack and runout as applicable.
Reverse-input drum, band and servo2nd and 4th apply/release
  • Measure drum scoring, checking, taper and runout.
  • Inspect band lining/anchors and case anchor.
  • Inspect servo bores, pin, pistons, seals, cushion spring and cover; measure installed travel.
  • Confirm accumulator and servo-release circuits meter and exhaust correctly.
Geartrain and one-way clutchesShell · splines · thrust system
  • Use a compatible hardened shell; inspect sun gear and reaction shaft.
  • Measure pinion endplay; inspect gears, pins, carriers, ring gear, output and parking parts.
  • Inspect every bushing, bearing and washer against the location map.
  • Verify sprag/roller races and lock/freewheel direction twice.
Valve body and electronicsVacuum test · resistance · compatibility
  • Vacuum-test applicable AFL, shift, accumulator, abuse and TCC-related bores.
  • Inspect plate seats/orifices, gaskets and casting channels.
  • Test solenoids and harness at specified temperature; inspect every terminal.
  • Keep pump, valve body, plate, harness, pressure switch and PCM strategy compatible.
09

Module build

Finish and prove each assembly first

Each clutch drum is clean, measured, lubricated and air-tested before it enters the case.

A

Low / reverse

  1. Install seals in documented direction.
  2. Install piston/springs with the correct compressor.
  3. Load wave, steels and frictions in 1994 sequence.
  4. Set and record clearance.
  5. Air-check apply/release.
B

Rear geartrain

  1. Install directional thrust and output parts.
  2. Install planetary/one-way parts.
  3. Seat support, anti-clunk spring and snap ring.
  4. Install sun gear and hardened shell.
  5. Verify rotation/direction after each layer.
C

Input housing

  1. Install seals with protectors.
  2. Build overrun and forward sprag; verify direction.
  3. Build/measure forward clutch.
  4. Build matched 3-4 system with specified springs.
  5. Measure around the pack, size rings and air-check.
  6. Leak-test the shaft joint if serviced.
D

Reverse-input drum

  1. Install protected piston seals/piston.
  2. Install retainer and correct snap ring.
  3. Orient wave/dish parts from 1994 data.
  4. Load plates/frictions and record travel.
  5. Air-check.
E

Pump

  1. Build the verified non-PWM pump.
  2. Use one documented pressure calibration.
  3. Confirm free valve/slide movement.
  4. Align halves with a band/guide bolts.
  5. Torque evenly; install correct seals/selective.
F

Valve body / accumulators

  1. Complete testing/bore repair first.
  2. Install matched plate/gaskets.
  3. Place balls from the verified map.
  4. Install stable pistons, seals and selected springs.
  5. Do not block vents without exact instructions.
What the video demonstrates well: seal protectors, nested-piston support, snap-ring inspection, sprag handling, loaded-drum air checks, pump alignment and a fluid/bubble check at the input-shaft joint. Replace improvised gauges with documented measurements.
10

Case stack-up

Use verification gates

If an assembly does not seat with alignment, hand pressure and controlled rotation, stop and find the interference.

  1. Build the rear stack.Lubricate thrust surfaces, confirm directions, seat snap rings and verify rotation/one-way action after each part.
  2. Install shell and drums.Engage every spline fully. Measure and compare drum depth.
  3. Install band and servo.Confirm anchors, new seals, cushion system and measured pin/travel.
  4. Install pump.Use guide studs and correct seals. Never pull a high pump down with bolts.
  5. Set endplay.Torque pump, measure total endplay with a dial indicator and correct with the specified selective.
  6. Install controls.Recheck balls, plate/gaskets, bolt lengths, manual link, accumulators, harness and pressure switch.
  7. Torque methodically.Use the exact sequence and a calibrated inch-pound wrench. Mark each group.
  8. Final internal audit.Confirm connections, filter seal/filter, manual ranges, rotation, records and no loose parts.
11

Vehicle installation

The converter and cooler are part of the rebuild

A clean transmission can fail immediately when connected to contaminated or restricted components.

01Converter

Use a properly remanufactured 298 mm, non-PWM Roadmaster/LT1 unit with verified pilot, pattern, hub and stall.

02Full engagement

Lubricate, rotate and push through turbine, stator and pump drive. Measure installed depth.

03Cooler circuit

Flush correctly, inspect discharge and perform a documented flow test. Replace uncertain components.

04Mate without force

Align dowels and seat the case by hand. Never use bellhousing bolts to close a gap.

05Converter clearance

Before pulling forward, verify specified converter-to-flexplate clearance and free rotation.

06Restore the vehicle

Torque all fasteners to 1994 values. Reconnect grounds, linkage, VSS, vent and original connectors.

Stop if the case will not sit flush. An incompletely engaged converter can break the pump as bellhousing bolts are tightened. Wrong pull-out clearance can damage the pump, converter or engine thrust bearing.
12

First fluid and motion

Prove the rebuild at low load

The Roadmaster manual lists about 10.6 L / 22.4 pt (11.2 qt) for an overhaul, but actual fill varies. Add incrementally and set final level by the dipstick procedure.

Fluid: the 1994 manual specified DEXRON-III or DEXRON-IIE. Those licenses are obsolete; choose a reputable current fluid explicitly recommended for the original GM application and compatible with the converter supplier. Nominal dry capacity is not a fill-to quantity.
