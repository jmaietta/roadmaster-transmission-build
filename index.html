01 · Scope

Compatibility comes before parts ordering

This plan targets the transmission shown in the FSP video: a 2001-style, 300 mm, removable-bellhousing 4L60E intended for an LS drivetrain. It does not describe the original 1994 Roadmaster LT1 transmission as an interchangeable assembly.

Critical: Do not mix the original 298 mm LT1 pump, stator, input shaft and converter family with the later 300 mm LS family. The case, valve body, separator plate, gaskets, harness, converter and PCM strategy must be treated as compatible systems.

Target configuration

  • 2001-style 4L60E architecture
  • Removable LS bellhousing
  • 300 mm pump, stator and converter family
  • 2WD output shaft and extension housing
  • PWM torque-converter-clutch control
  • Matching LS PCM or transmission controller

Identify before buying

  • Broadcast tag and case casting
  • Bellhousing casting and bolt pattern
  • Valve-body casting and separator plate
  • Input-shaft and output-shaft configuration
  • Internal harness and connector arrangement
  • Converter dimensions, pilot and bolt circle

02 · Source transmission

What failed in the video unit

The 260,000-mile video core suffered a broad mechanical and hydraulic failure. It was not a simple friction-and-seal overhaul.

Mechanical damage

  • Reaction shell splines completely stripped
  • Sun gear damaged
  • Lower planetary badly worn
  • Ring gear/reaction-shaft damage
  • Reverse-input drum replaced

Clutch and contamination

  • Forward clutch damaged
  • Metal circulated through the unit
  • Pump stator visibly scored
  • Converter and cooler circuit presumed contaminated

Hydraulic wear

  • AFL valve circuit failed vacuum test
  • TCC regulator circuit failed vacuum test
  • Remanufactured pump installed
  • Sonnax-remanufactured valve body installed
Build implication: Treat an unknown core as a complete teardown, cleaning, hydraulic-restoration and measured-reassembly project. Do not assume a master kit alone constitutes a rebuild.

03 · Specification

Recommended Roadmaster build objective

Build an OEM-plus, heavy-street transmission: strong enough for the Roadmaster’s mass and a stock or mildly modified 5.3-liter LS, but calibrated to avoid harsh driveline shock.

Priorities

  • Leak-free 3-4 clutch feed circuit
  • Stable, progressive line pressure
  • Strong reaction-shell and geartrain durability
  • Full-thickness clutch steels with thermal capacity
  • Firm, controlled shifts with retained cushioning
  • Proper converter clutch and cooling strategy

Not the objective

  • Maximum-pressure drag-race calibration
  • Largest possible second servo
  • Every accumulator blocked
  • Maximum clutch count using very thin steels
  • Loose high-stall converter with the 2.91 axle
  • Parts selected solely by marketing claims

05 · Guardrails

Modifications and shortcuts to avoid

Do not copy blindly

  • Do not use a dime as the final 3-4 clutch measurement.
  • Do not accept “it spins” as the only clutch-clearance test.
  • Do not tighten pump or valve-body bolts by feel.
  • Do not copy checkball placement from an unrelated year.
  • Do not install a different-year valve body without documented compatibility.

Do not over-calibrate

  • Do not block an accumulator passage unless the exact kit instructions require it.
  • Do not plug the hidden third-accumulator checkball circuit.
  • Do not combine maximum boost, maximum servo and aggressive PCM pressure tables indiscriminately.
  • Do not block the PWM TCC regulator circuit to force full-pressure lockup.
  • Do not remove all shift cushioning from a heavy street car.
Video-specific caution: The builder used a 0.500-inch boost valve, blocked an accumulator-related passage and installed a specially remanufactured 1995 valve body in a 2001 transmission. Those choices are valid only as parts of documented, matched calibrations.

Measurement

  • Digital multimeter
  • Bidirectional scan tool or suitable PCM data logger
A DIY builder without valve-body vacuum-testing and reaming equipment should send the cleaned valve body and pump to a transmission specialist. New clutches cannot compensate for worn AFL, pressure-regulator or TCC bores.

09 · Quality control

Bench-release gates

The pan should not be closed and the transmission should not be released for installation until every gate below is complete.

Hydraulic integrity

  • Pump circuits vacuum-tested
  • Valve body vacuum-tested
  • Third-accumulator checkball tested
  • Input-drum feed joint leak-tested
  • All clutch circuits air-checked

Mechanical integrity

  • One-way clutches verified
  • Geartrain rotates without unexplained bind
  • Band anchors and servo verified
  • All snap rings fully seated
  • Converter engagement depth measured

Measurements

  • Every clutch clearance recorded
  • Servo travel recorded
  • Planetary wear/endplay recorded
  • Total transmission endplay recorded
  • Torque sheet completed

Electrical and controls

  • Harness continuity confirmed
  • Solenoids tested
  • Pressure switch inspected
  • Manual valve movement confirmed
  • PCM calibration plan documented

10 · Commissioning

Installation, controller setup and first test

Roadmaster-specific controller considerations

  • Program the actual 2.91 final-drive ratio and rear tire diameter.
  • Match TCC strategy to the PWM converter and valve body.
  • Avoid commanding lockup at high load and very low engine speed.
  • Use progressive line-pressure tables rather than maximum pressure everywhere.
  • Set part-throttle and wide-open-throttle shift points to the actual engine combination.
  • Enable transmission-temperature protection and data logging.
