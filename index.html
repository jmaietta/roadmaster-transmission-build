01

Identity

Confirm exactly what is on the bench

The plan assumes the original-type 1994 B-body 4L60-E behind the Roadmaster-spec LT1. The first job is to prove that assumption before ordering any calibration-sensitive part.

Do not order a “1993–2006 4L60-E kit” and assume every part will fit.

The 1994 unit predates the 1995 PWM TCC system and the later 300 mm/removable-bellhousing configuration shown in the video. Pump, valve body, separator plate, gaskets, harness, solenoid arrangement and torque converter must remain a compatible 1994 system.

  • Tag, broadcast code, case casting and tailhousing photographed.
  • One-piece 1994 case and 2WD output configuration confirmed.
  • Original-style non-PWM valve-body layout confirmed.
  • 298 mm converter family confirmed from application and physical ID.
  • Separator plate and gasket package identified before disassembly.
  • Build date checked against 1994 garage-shift service update.
02

Build definition

Build for the Roadmaster

The wagon is heavy, the LT1 makes strong low-speed torque, and the factory calibration was designed for smooth Buick operation. The target is durability with controlled shifts, not maximum apply pressure and violent shift feel.

Build priorities

  • Preserve 1994 non-PWM electronics and TCC control.
  • Restore pump and valve-body hydraulic integrity.
  • Strengthen the reaction shell and 3-4 clutch system.
  • Retain clutch-steel thermal mass.
  • Retain appropriate accumulator and servo cushioning.
  • Control temperature with a completely clean cooler circuit.

What this build is not

  • No 2001 pump, valve body or PWM hardware conversion.
  • No maximum-pressure “race” calibration by default.
  • No arbitrary accumulator blocking.
  • No thin-steel clutch-count contest.
  • No converter reuse after metallic contamination.
  • No unmeasured clutch packs or endplay.
Recommended build level:

Stock-to-mild LT1, heavy-street use, highway cruising and normal wagon duty. A future high-output engine changes the input-shaft, drum, converter and calibration decisions and should be specified before parts are purchased.

Source control

1994 vs. 2001 video carry-overs

The mechanical architecture is similar enough that the video is valuable. The year-dependent hydraulic and electrical details are different enough that copying them directly can ruin the transmission.

System 1994 Roadmaster build 2001 video unit Rule for this project
TCC control Early non-PWM/on-off strategy PWM-controlled Retain complete 1994 strategy; no PWM.
Converter family 298 mm 300 mm Buy LT1-compatible 298 mm converter.
Case One-piece early case Later removable bellhousing Ignore later bellhousing assumptions.
Pump 1993–94 non-PWM 300 mm pump w/ TransGo Rebuild verified non-PWM pump.
Valve body 1994 calibration, plate, gaskets Specially remanufactured casting Use a 1994-correct assembly.
Checkballs Follow 1994 map Later arrangement Use video for handling only, not locations.
Accumulator mod Keep 1994 street cal Builder blocks passage Do not copy unless 1994 kit instructs.
1994 service-update trap:

Transmissions built before March 7, 1994 may have the original garage-shift package. GM required the spacer plate and garage-shift package to remain matched. Do not mix an updated package with the original plate or the reverse.

09

Rebuild

Assemble and test modules

Each clutch drum is a finished, measured, air-tested component before installation. “It spins” and “the kit predetermines it” are not acceptable final measurements.

C. Input housing: overrun, forward and 3-4 clutches

  1. Apply Loctite to the input shaft splines/base to seal the 3-4 clutch circuit from leaking.
  2. Install updated long-lip forward and 3-4 seals with cone protectors (Lip Wizard) while carefully applying simultaneous pressure.
  3. Install forward-housing O-ring and all pistons, apply shells and return springs.
  4. Assemble overrun clutch and forward sprag; strictly verify clockwise freewheel direction.
  5. Assemble forward clutch and measure travel/clearance.
  6. Assemble the matched heavy-duty 3-4 clutch system, including all load-release springs.
  7. Measure 3-4 travel around the circumference with a feeler gauge; check plate flatness and snap-ring seating.
  8. Install and size the input-shaft Teflon sealing rings with dedicated tools.
  9. Air-check overrun, forward and 3-4 circuits.
  10. If the shaft joint was resealed, fluid-test around the joint and confirm zero bubbles.

Use from the video: seal protectors, simultaneous support of nested pistons, sprag handling, loaded-drum air checks and the fluid/bubble check at the input shaft. Improve on the video: use feeler gauges/dial measurement and a written build sheet—not a dime or hand feel as the final acceptance test.
